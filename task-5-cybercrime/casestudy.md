# Cyberbullying

## 📌 What is the Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where an individual or group uses digital platforms (social media, messaging apps, gaming platforms, etc.) to threaten, humiliate, or harm another person. It is considered a serious offense and may be punishable under cyber laws depending on the severity.

---

## 🔄 How Cyberbullying Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **Target Selection**  
   The bully identifies a person (often someone perceived as vulnerable).

2. **Initial Contact or Trigger**  
   It may start with a disagreement, jealousy, or simply for entertainment.

3. **Harassment Begins**  
   The bully sends hurtful messages, posts abusive comments, or spreads rumors.

4. **Public Exposure**  
   Harmful content (images, videos, personal information) may be shared publicly.

5. **Escalation**  
   The bullying becomes frequent, involving threats, fake accounts, or group attacks.

6. **Victim Impact**  
   The target experiences emotional distress, anxiety, or social withdrawal.

---

## 🎯 Who is Usually Targeted?

- Students who are shy, introverted, or new to a group  
- Individuals with different appearances, opinions, or backgrounds  
- People who are socially isolated or lack strong support systems  
- Victims of previous bullying (online or offline)  
- Public figures or active social media users  

---

## ⚠️ Consequences of Cyberbullying

### For the Victim:
- Emotional distress, anxiety, and depression  
- Low self-esteem and loss of confidence  
- Academic decline or social withdrawal  
- In severe cases, self-harm or suicidal thoughts  

### For the Bully:
- Legal consequences (fines, criminal charges)  
- School or college disciplinary actions  
- Damage to reputation and future opportunities  

### For Society:
- Creates a toxic online environment  
- Reduces trust and safety in digital communities  

---

## 💡 Final Note

Cyberbullying is not "just a joke"—it can have serious real-life consequences.  
Always choose kindness and report harmful behavior when you see it.
