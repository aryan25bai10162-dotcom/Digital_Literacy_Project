## Impact of Poor Digital Communication

A situation where poor digital communication caused a problem is when a student emailed a professor using informal language and unclear instructions, such as “Send me the notes ASAP.” The message lacked a proper greeting, context, and politeness, which made it sound rude and confusing. As a result, the professor ignored the email, and the student missed important study material before exams.

This issue could have been avoided by following proper email etiquette. The student should have used a clear subject line, a respectful greeting, and a polite tone. For example, clearly mentioning the request, course details, and reason would have made the communication more effective. Proper digital communication ensures clarity, professionalism, and better responses.
